A bit of history

La Union was created on March 2, 1850, when a Spanish decree joined together the southern towns of Ilocos Sur, the northern towns of Pangasinan, and a few settlements near the Cordillera. That merging of territories is where the province got its name. San Fernando was made the capital, and today La Union is best known as the surfing capital of the North.
